As a last semester project, we were asked to make games with interactive devices in groups of 4. We had 2 months to prepare these games for a presentation night. I had the role of artist and game designer.

Game design

In the heart of a room equipped with multiple interactive devices, 2 to 3 players engage in a quest while playing a series of mini-games. Like Wario Ware, the intensity of games will gradually increase using a timer. Players have 3 lives, losing them if they fail a mini-game, and regaining them by defeating a boss.

The games require players to move around the room due to the timer and specific technology locations. The main quest, linked to the mini-games, may be paused or slowed down, forcing players to solve the problem with a particular mini-game. The main quest is managed using a projector, displaying instructions for the mini-games.

Art Direction

For the artistic direction (which was followed in all aspects of the game) we chose with the other designer to start with a sort of patchwork of graphic style (like the game which is a patchwork of mini games!). It was first of all a choice in line with the game design, and also a choice of ease because there was no real need to adapt to everyone's style to have coherence in the game. We made difference, chaos and disorder our style, and it worked perfectly.
